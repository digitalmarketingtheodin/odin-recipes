Pad Gra Pow

Description

Pad gra pow is a traditional Thai dish that is very popular both in Thailand and around the world. It is my families personal favorite recipe, and the version we like best involves frying ground pork in garlic, chilis, and basil. When purchased from a street vendor or restuarant in thailand it is common for it to be served with a friedegg on top - which we do by filling a wok with oil, heating it to frying temperature and cracking an egg right into it.

Ingredients

  • 2lbs Ground Pork
  • 1oz Thai Basil
  • 8 Cloves of Garlic
  • 3-4 Thai Chilis (to taste)
  • Fish Sauce
  • Oyster Sauce
  • Rice Vinegar
  • Soy Sauce
  • Brown Sugar
  • Onion
  • Long Bean
  • Red Bell Pepper
  • Cucumber
  • Lime
  • Cilantro
  • Peanut or Safflower oil

Directions

  1. Dice chilis and garlic, you can use a mortar and pestle to make paste if you wish Juliene the bell pepper and onion and cut the long bean into ~2cm long pieces Dice the basil and cilantro seperately. Cut limes into wedges and the cucumber into medium slices.
  2. Mix the sauce together by combining soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, rice vinegar, and brown sugar into a bowl and whisk until combined
  3. Heat wok on high and add the cooking oil.
  4. Add the garlic and chili paste, stir aggressively until fragrant and add the ground pork
  5. Once the pork has begun to render its own fat, add the chopped onions, bell pepper & long bean
  6. Cook for 1 or 2 minutes before adding the sauce
  7. Once the sauce is added, continue to stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes
  8. When everything is well mixed together and before the vegetables have gotten soft, remove from heat and add the basil
  9. Serve over Jasmine rice, with fried egg on top (optional), use the cucumber, cilantro and Lime as garnish
